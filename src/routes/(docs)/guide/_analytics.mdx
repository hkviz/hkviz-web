# Analytics Guide

This guide covers the different parts of the <HKVizText /> gameplay analytics page.
Open it from one of your gameplays on the <AA href="/">start page</AA> or from the <AA href="/run">public gameplays page</AA>.

If you want to learn how to record analytics while playing {<ExternalLinkMdx href="https://hollowknight.com">Hollow Knight</ExternalLinkMdx>},
check out the <AA href="/guide/install">Gameplay Recording Guide</AA>.

## Map

The map is the main part of the analytics page. It is similar to the in-game map, but includes a few helpful modifications.

It can show player movement as traces, visited rooms, and other stats by recoloring the map.

### <Spline class="w-4 h-4 mr-2 inline-block"/>Player movement and traces

<ReflowRow>
    <ReflowCell>
        <ReflowCellHeader>Animated</ReflowCellHeader>
        <TracesAnimatedScreenshot />
        Shows the last 5 minutes of player's movement as a trace. Positions further in the past are faded out.
    </ReflowCell>
    <ReflowCell>
        <ReflowCellHeader>All</ReflowCellHeader>
        <TracesAllScreenshot />
        Shows the player's movement of the complete gameplay at once.
    </ReflowCell>
    <ReflowCell>
        <ReflowCellHeader>Hidden</ReflowCellHeader>
        <TracesNoneScreenshot />
        Hides the trace. This can be useful, if you want to <a href="#map-coloring-by-variables" target="_self">look at a variable on the map</a>, without the trace covering it.
    </ReflowCell>

</ReflowRow>

### <LayoutDashboard class="w-4 h-4 mr-2 inline-block"/>Room visibility

Room visibility controls which rooms are shown.
Choose between three options:

<ReflowRow>
    <ReflowCell>
        <ReflowCellHeader>Animated</ReflowCellHeader>
        <RoomVisibilityAnimatedVideo />
		Rooms appear once they are visited in the gameplay, based on the time selected in the <a href="#timeline" target="_self">timeline</a>.

    	Useful for following the progression of a gameplay.
    </ReflowCell>
    <ReflowCell>
        <ReflowCellHeader>Visited</ReflowCellHeader>
        <RoomVisibilityVisitedVideo />
    	All rooms visited at any point in the gameplay are shown. Not affected by the <a href="#timeline" target="_self">timeline</a>.

    	Useful when you want to <a href="#map-coloring-by-variables" target="_self">analyze variables by recoloring the map</a>.
    </ReflowCell>
    <ReflowCell>
        <ReflowCellHeader>All</ReflowCellHeader>
        <RoomVisibilityAllVideo />
    	Even rooms that were never visited in the gameplay are shown.

    	Helpful for spotting rooms that were missed.
    </ReflowCell>

</ReflowRow>

### <Palette class="w-5 h-5 mr-2 inline-block"/>Map coloring by variables

<RoomAnalyticsScreenshot />

Coloring the map based on different game stats can help you spot interesting or difficult parts of a gameplay.

**How it works:**

1.  **Select a room** by hovering over it on the map. Its stats appear in the room analytics.

    Hint: the selected room is also highlighted in the <a href="#timeline" target="_self">timeline</a>.

2.  {<LinkableSpan id="room-pin" initialScrollMargin={360}>

    <b>Pin a room:</b> To keep the selected room from changing while you hover over other rooms, pin it with the

    {' '}

    <Pin class="inline-block h-4 w-4" aria-label="Pin" />
    button in the area analytics panel or by clicking a room on the map. You can also pin rooms by clicking the color
    code at the bottom of the
    <span> </span>
    <a href="#timeline" target="_self">
    	timeline
    </a>
    <span> or by double-clicking a </span>
    <a href="#splits" target="_self">
    	split
    </a>
    .

    </LinkableSpan>}

3.  **Select a variable for coloring**:
    Click the <Palette class="inline-block h-4 w-4" aria-label="Color" />
    button in the room's info panel to pick the stat used for coloring.

4.  **Color scales**:
    Clicking the <Palette class="inline-block h-4 w-4" aria-label="Color" />
    button cycles through the available color scales.

#### Color scales:

<ReflowRow>
    <ReflowCell>
        <ReflowCellHeader>Linear color scale</ReflowCellHeader>
        <ColorModeLinearTableScreenshot />
        <ColorModeLinearScreenshot />
		The first click on the <Palette class="w-4 h-4 inline-block" aria-label="Color" /> button colors the map by the selected variable using a linear scale.
		This works best for variables like first visiting time, where values are usually spread fairly evenly.
    </ReflowCell>
    <ReflowCell>
        <ReflowCellHeader>Exponential color scale</ReflowCellHeader>
        <ColorModeExpTableScreenshot />
        <ColorModeExpScreenshot />
		A second click on the <Palette class="w-4 h-4 inline-block" aria-label="Color" /> button switches to an exponential scale.

        For many variables, only a few rooms have high values while many rooms have low values.
        An exponential scale makes the low values easier to compare.
        This is especially useful for geo spent, since most geo is spent in only a small number of shops.
    </ReflowCell>
    <ReflowCell>
        <ReflowCellHeader>Area color</ReflowCellHeader>
        <ColorModeAreaTableScreenshot />
        <ColorModeAreaScreenshot />
    	Clicking the <Palette class="w-4 h-4 inline-block" aria-label="Color" /> button a third time switches back to coloring the map by area, matching the in-game map.
    </ReflowCell>

</ReflowRow>

Additional color scales are available from the dropdown menu in the top right of the map after selecting a variable with the <Palette class="w-4 h-4 inline-block" aria-label="Color" /> button.

### <Tent class="w-5 h-5 mr-2 inline-block" />Special rooms

The map in <HKVizText /> includes a few changes so more areas can be analyzed.
These include added maps for Godhome, White Palace, and Birthplace, plus a few other adjustments:

#### Buildings

<BuildingsColormapScreenshot />

Some parts of the Hollow Knight world are not shown directly on the map, but are hinted at by a building or door.
For <HKVizText /> these have been cut out from the game sprites so analytics for them can be shown individually.

In the game, the player location is hidden inside these buildings.
In <HKVizText /> the player movement is shown on top of them.

#### Multi-room buildings

<SubroomsScreenshot />

Some buildings contain multiple rooms. On the map, analytics are shown for all rooms inside the building together.
(For example, deaths are summed across all rooms, and "first visited at" uses the earliest room visit.)

Click the building on the map to view analytics for each room individually.

## Time-based charts

The time-based charts show variables over time, such as completion percentage, geo, and grubs collected.

Hovering over a chart shows a marker on the <a href="#timeline" target="_self">timeline</a>.
The player position is also highlighted on the <a href="#map" target="_self">map</a>.

## Timeline

The timeline lets you scroll through a gameplay and inspect player location and room visibility at different points in time.
On the right side, you can adjust the animation speed.

<TimelineScreenshot />

At the bottom of the timeline, the colors of visited scenes are shown. By <a href="#map-coloring-by-variables" target="_self">selecting a room on the map</a>, the color codes for the selected room and area are enlarged.
This makes it easier to see when a specific area was visited.
